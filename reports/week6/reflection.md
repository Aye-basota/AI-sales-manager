# Week 6 Reflection

## Learning points

- Trial readiness is not the same as final transition. The Week 6 call showed that a live walkthrough can prove product direction, but independent customer use still needs a concrete access artifact and written confirmation.
- Lead discovery is valuable but quality-sensitive. The feature produced visible candidates, yet the customer immediately evaluated usefulness by result quality rather than by whether the parser technically worked.
- Prompt quality remains a customer-facing reliability concern. Even when the main workflow works, occasional role-breaking output affects trust.
- Handover documentation must match operational truth. The Week 6 access story needs to distinguish the Telegram Admin Bot trial entry point from final customer-side deployment, which is still Week 7 work.

## Validated assumptions

- The customer cares most about reducing manual sales setup work and getting usable leads quickly.
- The Telegram Admin Bot remains the main customer-facing control surface for the current product.
- Public reports need to separate sanitized evidence from Moodle-only recordings, timecodes, credentials, and customer-identifying details.
